Streaming made every song available, but it did not make every scene visible. That is where the local music column still matters.

A good local column does not chase only the largest names. It notices the basement show, the self-released EP, the strange collaboration, the venue fighting to stay open, and the artist who has not yet learned how to market themselves.

Music culture is built through attention. When a city’s small publications disappear, the archive disappears with them.

The local music column is not nostalgia. It is infrastructure.
