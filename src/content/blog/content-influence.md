# Premise
Today we go through our lives consuming vast amounts of information. We read the news, scroll Instagram or Tiktok, watch series, etc and at least in my case I went about my life without realising how much this was affecting/influencing me. The truth is that every piece of information we take in affects us to some extent. No matter how small, with enough exposure it can influence you without you realising. Later in the post I will go over some examples where this can be really bad. On the other hand, being aware of this can give you a small super power to help you achieve your goals, and later I will show some examples to illustrate this.

# We consume much more than before
What is the normal amount of information to consume? I guess this question really has no answer as it depends on the context but it makes you wonder what did the average amount of information a human consumed has changed over time. At the start of the 20th century we really only had radio and paper. People mostly read books to consume information formally and radio for more quick information. In those times I also assume that other people where a big source of information, where you heard some gossip from neighbours or relatives.

Now taking a look at the world today, and we can see things have changed. Now radio and books are not so popular and media such as TV and smartphones have become the new normal. There is a big rabbit hole here where I could dive into comparing the different kinds of media consumption and how they affect us differently but I will save it for another post. The main conclusion from this is that these new information avenues we use are much more involved with our senses as such we make a stronger connection with the content we see.

## What connection do you make to the media?

Personally as I have experienced myself, media consumption in a way makes our brain imagine itself in a different position through empathy. Say we watch a video on homelessness and we feel sad. Our brain has built this empathetic connection with the information to the point it feels it should express an emotion of sadness. 

Videos can more easily create a stronger sense of empathy than text, and I suspect this is because we are exploiting human senses in a much deeper way than text. In a way even though you know that the video you are watching is just a video your brain thinks it is real (to a certain extent). To prove this I think a classic example is those videos that have a jump scare in order to prank your friends. In these videos the person watching is not expecting this scary moment but then suddenly the video puts a loud nose and an image flashes on the screen, which causes the observer to jump from their seat. Even though the person watching knew this was just a video their unconscious did not.

In this aspect I like to point out that even though our conscious brain has evolved and become more intelligent, our unconscious brain feels like it is still the same it was a thousand years ago.

# What do I mean by consumption?
When we humans consume information, we typically do it on purpose for example reading the News, looking at social media, etc. For a piece of information to cause some sort of change in our brains we only need to read it, see it or hear about it. That's it. We don't have to be conscious of the fact that it is causing some change and this is the nuance here.

> _In all the examples listed above you do not control or know what you are going to see._

This means that the news channel could choose to show you news that deeply depressed you, enraged you or made you happy, furthermore it could bias you and in turn changed your opinion about a topic. For example, if you haven't seen any news on Cyber crime and then suddenly you see the news around the [flipper zero](https://flipper.net/) and how it is causing chaos; you might immediately think that this device should be banned. Truth was that news outlets on this device exploited very few examples of its malicious use and changed how many people thought. This lead to this device being banned in some countries.

I guess the main point here is that by choosing to use these avenues of information you are letting the door open to be influenced by them. And this is completely fine. Another avenue of information consumption is the people around us, like parents, friends, colleagues, etc. This is where the famous saying comes up, "if you surround yourself with idiots you will become an idiot". In other words by being in the same space and seeing people act you are letting them influence you ever so slightly.
